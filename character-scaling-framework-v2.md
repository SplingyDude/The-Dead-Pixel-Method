# The Substrate-Proximity Framework
### A structural map — v2, the *On Writing* pass

**Version:** 2.0 · **Revised:** 2026-05-31
**What changed:** Feats cut. Every entry now answers *what it is for in the story*, not *what it can destroy*. Tier letters retired in favor of the ring architecture underneath them. Trimmed about a tenth, per King's second-draft math. The darlings — the feats — went first.

> Working rule, after King: don't ask how strong a thing is. Ask what it's about. A character who is only feats is an adverb with a name.

---

## 1. First principle

Power doesn't decide outcomes. **Relationship to the substrate does** — how close a thing sits to the code reality is rendered from, and what access it holds there.

The corollary is the spine of the cosmology: **the top of the ladder can't be climbed.** Every rung below the kernel is won by escalation. The kernel is won by relationship, or not at all.

## 2. The ring architecture

The ladder is CPU protection rings — Ring 3 at the rim, Ring 0 at the center, each ring trusting only the one beneath it. Read it as access *and* as narrative role.

- **Ring 3 — userland (versal).** The threat that looks final and isn't. Fights inside the rules. The opponent the protagonist outgrows.
- **sudo — Admin-class.** Competence. The working self: enough power to matter, not enough to cheat.
- **Ring 0, on a server — Root / Developer.** The power that solves by force and pays the bill. The temptation the story has to refuse.
- **The kernel — Yog-Sothoth.** The problem no credential opens. The gate that is also the key. Met, not beaten.

Two functions act *on* the rings instead of occupying one:

- **Entropy** — the corruption that makes the substrate mortal. Without it, nothing is at stake.
- **Control** — the hand that moves players without touching the board.

## 3. The classes, by function

| Class | What it's for |
|---|---|
| Versal | The outgrown threat |
| Entropy | The reason stakes are real |
| Control | The unseen hand |

| Hybrid | A boundary marker: one borrowed trait on a lesser chassis |

## 4. Interaction rules

- **R1.** A thing that edits the rules beats a thing that only fights inside them.
- **R2.** Living off the map is not the same as winning by off-map rules.
- **R3.** Corruption is *stalled* by containment from a higher frame; *ended* only by legitimate authority that revokes its access.
- **R4.** Authority is bounded by jurisdiction. Root on your own server is not root on the network.
- **R5.** Deletion needs something to delete. A thing that re-knits, or is already dead, offers nothing to take.
- **R6.** Manipulation needs a will to work on. The mindless can't be bargained with — and can't be talked down either.
- **R7.** The kernel is befriended, not beaten. Once the apex is defined as the thing nothing sits above, the only move left is off the power axis entirely. You make a friend, because there is no gun for this.

## 5. The roster, by function

Each entry: source · ring/class · what it's *for* · the limit that defines it.

**Shin Akuma / Oni** — *Street Fighter* · Versal. The finisher that proves a rule: an output fighter whose one relevant trait is that he aims at the soul, not the body. *Limit:* nothing to aim at, once the target re-knits.

**Meta-Doomslayer** — *DOOM* · Versal. The unkillable forward motion; the thing containment can't hold. *Limit:* he can break any cell and open no lock.

**Sun Wukong** — *Journey to the West* · Hybrid. The trickster who edited himself out of one rule and thinks that's all of them. *Limit:* a frame he can't leap clear of, however far the jump.

**Tabuu** — *Smash Bros.* · Hybrid. The landlord of a void who still fights like a tenant. *Limit:* the address isn't the authority.

**The astras** — Hindu cosmology · spans the ladder. An arsenal that contains its own refutation: the surrender-weapon that beats resistance, and the exit (moksha) that beats the board. *Limit:* as force, still only force.

**The Mātṛkās** — Shakta theology · Transcendence. The ground wearing a face: power that is an *aspect* of the substrate, not a draw upon it. *Limit:* bound to their role as facets, not the whole.

**MissingNo.** — *Pokémon* · Entropy. The error that eats the record. *Limit:* mindless — it spreads, it doesn't aim.

**G-Man** — *Half-Life* · Control. The hand that files you away between uses. *Limit:* needs a will to grip and a jurisdiction to stand in.

**The Great Filter / Nyarlathotep** — Fermi / Lovecraft · forks. The silence, given either no face (entropy) or the one cruel face (control). *Limit:* still penultimate. Something dreams above it.

**Azathoth** — Lovecraft · Transcendence (ceiling). The dreamer the world is contingent on. *Limit:* a ceiling — and ceilings break from above, at a cost the story counts.

**Mathrus** — M.U.G.E.N legend · Entropy (apex). Proof the substrate is mortal all the way down to the host. Defined as much by its own unverifiability as by its reach: a threat that may be only a rumor, which is its own kind of power. *Limit:* a frame it can't corrupt holds it; an authority above it ends it.

**Yog-Sothoth** — *INFINITE NOTHINGS*, Book Two · Kernel. The apex no credential opens. Not malevolent — an addict, hoarding lossless prime information, the reserve currency of reality in its purest form. *Resolution:* an intervention run by someone who earned the standing to run it. Met with a friend, not a gun.

**Sean Firekind**, by build —

- *Cyrus (Admin / sudo).* The competent working self. Reality-editing within limits.
- *NIGHTMARE (released limiter).* The spike that costs exactly what it spends — power without growth. The temptation.
- *Developer (Root).* The credential that solves by force. Book One's answer; Book Two's regret.
- *First-prestige pistol-start (Book Two).* The de-scaled self. Hands root back, restarts at userland with a sidearm, because the last problem can't be solved with access. The admin gun's new **trigger** is the whole arc in one part: the old one fired on impulse and couldn't hold a ready stance; the new one lets him aim and *choose not to*. Two kinds of aim — reflex and deliberation — are two kinds of person. *Function:* the capacity to not fire.

## 6. Open questions

- **Transcendence vs. Control.** If the cruel face is the dreamer's own messenger, is it a tool of the ceiling or a parasite on it?
- **The relational apex.** R7 says the kernel is befriended. Does that retroactively mark every *kill* beneath it — Azathoth included — as the lesser path? The framework now suspects it does.

## 7. Glossary

*Versal* — raw output; the floor. *Substrate* — the code reality renders from. *Ring levels* — access measured as CPU protection rings; the ladder's true shape. *Private domain vs. private rules* — living off-map vs. winning by it. *Containment vs. revocation* — stalling corruption vs. ending it. *Jurisdiction* — the scope an authority holds. *The kernel* — Ring 0 of all reality; befriended, not beaten.

## 8. Provenance

A story is found, not built — King's fossil, not a machine.
