## Premise

Reality renders almost perfectly. Almost. There is one dead pixel — a single null point, a place where the signal never arrived. The Theory is the practice of treating that null not as a defect to hide, but as the **gap where meaning is made.**

You don't fix the dead pixel. You fire across it.

## The Engine (ported)

The Theory runs on one mechanism, and it's the same engine that powers the whole project: **the substrate is never the source.**

A synapse is the gap between two neurons; meaning doesn't live in either cell, it *fires across the space between them.* The dead pixel is that gap, drawn as a single black point. Nothing lives *in* it — which is precisely why **your** charge has to fire across it for anything to ignite. The text is the inert vessel. Your associations are the lens. The meaning is the spark in the gap, and it is **yours.**

Three layers, reframed:

1. **BIOS — the Dead Pixel.** The null. The honest starting axiom: *there is nothing here but what you bring.*
2. **Kernel — your charge.** Your own loaded associations, fired across the gap. This is the lens. This is where the Theory actually lives.
3. **Recompile — re-resonance.** The charged image surfaces later, recombined, in something *you* think or make, and seeds the next reader.

It runs **memetically** — it propagates by re-firing through whatever you already carry, a little differently in every reader — and **open-label** — you can know exactly what it's doing and it still works, because the effect was never built on deceiving you. It was built on charging you.
