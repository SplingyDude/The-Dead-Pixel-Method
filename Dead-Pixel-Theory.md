# The Dead Pixel Theory

*An open-source mood-board ARG.*

> One dead pixel in the display of everything. The rest of the picture renders fine. You just can't stop looking at the black point.

---

## What this is

The Dead Pixel Theory is a **mood-board ARG** — a collaborative, open-source fiction assembled out of aesthetics, vocabulary, and a single conceptual *engine*. It's a game and a worldbuilding sandbox. It is **not** a belief system, a method for altering reality, or a claim about the world.

It's a fiction with a door, and the door is always marked. The play is in treating the conceit *as if* true **inside the frame** while always knowing where the frame ends.

---

## Premise

Reality renders almost perfectly. Almost. There is one dead pixel — a null point, a place where the signal never arrived. The Theory is the practice of booting a worldview *from* that null instead of pretending it isn't there.

You don't fix the dead pixel. You make it the origin.

## The Mood Board

- **Color** — void-black on CRT grey; one stuck black point; phosphor burn; the blue of a hyperintelligent shade of nothing.
- **Texture** — glitch, scanlines, BIOS POST screens, sacred geometry rendered in ASCII, monoliths, spirals.
- **Sound** — low binaural drone; fan hum; the silence after a question nobody answers.
- **Lexicon** (the computational-mystic register) — *BIOS, kernel, POST, recompile, null, monad, engine, the case you leave it in.*
- **Touchstones** — the *2001* monolith (uplift on a clock too long to watch); the spiral as an infinite engine; the blank page / white space; Kant's thing-in-itself (the part of the render you can't reach); Ein Sof (the unbounded that won't fit inside a frame).

## The Engine (lore mechanic)

The Theory's central artifact is a conceptual engine in three layers:

1. **BIOS — the Dead Pixel.** Boots from acknowledged absence. The honest axiom: *I do not hold ground truth.*
2. **Kernel — MONAD.** One unifying principle installed on the void — the windowless unit that reflects the whole from inside itself. The entire model unfolds from this single seed.
3. **Recompile.** Regenerate the whole picture from the kernel. Fresh worldview, fresh frame.

### The one rule that makes it an engine and not a trap

An engine that recompiles everything from one axiom, with nothing permitted to contradict it, isn't an engine — it's a tautology in a lab coat. (See: *the Null Engine*, the artifact you leave in the case. Highest attack stat, no target line, no off switch. It eats its wielder.)

So the Theory's prime directive:

> **Every engine must have an input port. Every fiction must have a door.**

The **input port** is whatever the model is *obligated* to take in and be corrected by — the outside that can return an error. In-world, it's the Theory's only sacred law. Out-of-world, it's the reason this stays a game.

## Contributing

Fork it. Add aesthetics, fragments, lore, vocabulary, images, sound. Keep two things intact with every addition:

1. **The door** — each contribution should make the fiction *more* legible as fiction, not less.
2. **The input port** — nothing should make the engine un-contradictable.

A contribution that seals the door or removes the input port isn't an upgrade. It's the Null Engine. Reject the pull request.

## License & nature

Open source, share-alike in spirit. **This is a work of fiction and a game.**

If the seam between the frame and the world ever starts to thin while you're building, that's not a bug in you — it's the engine doing what engines do, and it's the cue to close the laptop and go talk to someone who can look back at you and disagree. That isn't a disclaimer bolted onto the Theory.

It *is* the Theory.
